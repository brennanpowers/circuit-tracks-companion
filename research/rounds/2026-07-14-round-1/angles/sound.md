## Circuit Tracks — Sound Engine Field Notes (all from official Circuit Tracks User Guide v3 unless flagged)

Source of truth: the Circuit Tracks User Guide Version 3 PDF (Focusrite/Novation). Every page I cite is explicitly Circuit Tracks, not the original Circuit or Rhythm. Reviewer material (Sound On Sound, DJTechTools) used only to corroborate engine heritage and to sanity-check jack types — where the manual and a reviewer disagree, the manual wins (see Outputs).

### The two synth tracks
Two synth tracks, **Synth 1** and **Synth 2**, operate identically; the only difference is pad colour — Synth 1 keys are violet, Synth 2 keys are pale green (high/low notes of each octave are paler). Both are **six-note polyphonic**: you can assign up to six notes to any single step, provided the patch is polyphonic (some patches are mono). Manual (p27, p35) calls the engine "refined, polyphonic digital synth." SoS review identifies these as the **Nova engines** — dual-oscillator wavetable/virtual-analogue polysynths, the same engine family as the original Circuit and MiniNova/Nova heritage — but the manual itself never prints the word "Nova," so treat the exact heritage label as reviewer-sourced (medium confidence), while the "digital, dual-oscillator, six-voice poly, 128 patches" facts are manual-confirmed.

**Patch selection on-grid**: press the **Preset** button to open Patch View. Each synth has **128 factory patches** developed specifically for Circuit Tracks, arranged as **four pages of 32**. Pressing Preset with Synth 1 selected opens Patch View Page 1; with Synth 2 selected it opens Page 3. Navigate pages with the **▼/▲ arrow buttons** (their brightness indicates the current page). The active patch's pad lights white; others light violet (S1) or pale green (S2). Default patch is **Patch 1** (Page 1 Pad 1) for Synth 1 and **Patch 33** (Page 2 Pad 1) for Synth 2. You can change patch while a pattern plays (transition may not be seamless). **Patch Preview**: patches audition on selection, respecting the current scale/root; **hold Shift while pressing a patch pad to disable preview** (also auto-disabled in Record Mode during playback). Patches can also be recalled by external MIDI **Program Change** on Channel 1 (Synth 1) / Channel 2 (Synth 2) by default.

**The 8 macros**: eight endless rotary encoders with RGB LEDs (violet/pale-green per selected synth; brightness tracks value). Each macro can tweak **up to four patch parameters** at once. Default printed labels on the panel: **1 Oscillator, 2 Oscillator Mod, 3 Amp Envelope, 4 Filter Envelope, 5 Filter Frequency, 6 Resonance, 7 Modulation, 8 FX**. But the manual explicitly warns these are only the "primary function" and that specific patches — especially ones made in Components — reassign macros to entirely different functions. **On-device editing is limited to turning those 8 macros** (no from-scratch synthesis on the hardware). **Deep editing lives in Novation Components' Synth Editor**: there you assign any four parameters per knob with individual range and depth, build patches from scratch, and load them onto the unit. Macro moves can be recorded as automation in Record Mode (LED turns red on turn); delete automation by holding **Clear** + turning the macro (LED flashes red).

### Scales and keys
**Up to 16 scales**, set in **Scales View** (press the **Scales** button). Scale list on pads 17–32: 17 Natural Minor, 18 Major, 19 Dorian, 20 Phrygian, 21 Mixolydian, 22 Melodic Minor (ascending), 23 Harmonic Minor, 24 Bebop Dorian, 25 Blues, 26 Minor Pentatonic, 27 Hungarian Minor, 28 Ukranian Dorian, 29 Marva, 30 Todi, 31 Whole Tone, 32 Chromatic. **Only Chromatic has more than eight notes (12 notes)**; all others have eight or fewer. The lower two rows (17–32) select the scale; the upper two rows are the **keynote/root selection**, laid out like one octave of a piano (Row 2 = white notes, Row 1 = black notes; pads 1, 4, 8, 16 are always disabled to make the black-note layout work). **Default root is C** (Pad 9, lit darker blue). Press a different keynote pad to transpose. Keynote pads always show an octave from C to B.

In **Note View** the two lower rows are the synth keyboard; the upper row sits one octave above the lower row, and the top note of the lower octave equals the bottom note of the upper octave (Pad 32 = Pad 17). Total synth range is **10 octaves**, reached in octave pairs with ▼/▲. **Chromatic is the exception** — with 12 notes it only fits a one-octave keyboard in Note View. **Expanded Note View** (hold Shift + press Note; pads turn gold, or press Note again to toggle) removes the step display and gives you the **next two higher octaves** — effectively a four-octave (one-octave for Chromatic) playing surface, useful for real-time recording. Changing scale or root is **non-destructive and works while playing**: notes absent from the new scale are intelligently remapped (usually a semitone up/down), and reverting restores the originals.

### The four drum tracks
Four independent drum tracks, **Drum 1–4** (each a discrete sequencer track — a Tracks upgrade over the original Circuit's paired lanes). Each track can use any one of **64 pre-loaded samples**. In Note View the samples appear as **four pages of 16** (page shown by ▼/▲ brightness); in **Preset View** (press the track button, then **Preset**) they appear as **two pages of 32**. Default sample allocation: **Drum 1 = Page 1 slot 1 (Kick 1); Drum 2 = Page 1 slot 3 (Snare 1); Drum 3 = Page 1 slot 5 (Closed hi-hat 1); Drum 4 = Page 1 slot 9 (Additional percussion 1)**. Each page is a "kit" laid out consistently: slots 1–2 kicks, 3–4 snares, 5–6 closed hats, 7–8 open hats, 9–12 additional percussion, 13–16 melodic sounds.

**Sample selection gesture**: a quick **tap** on a sample pad changes the active sample for that track; a **long press** plays the sample but leaves the previous one active (lets you play a one-off hit without reassigning the track). **Sample Flip** = per-step sample assignment that overcomes the one-sample-per-track limit: hit **Record** and play different samples live, OR press-and-hold a sample pad (it turns red) then press the step pads where you want it (they turn red). Flipped steps illuminate **pink** in the pattern display; normal active-sample hits illuminate **bright blue**. Can be done independently on all four drum tracks. **Expanded Drum View** (Shift + Note): pads **29–32** become live triggers for Drums 1–4 simultaneously (all other pads disabled).

**Drum macros**: only the **even-numbered macros (2, 4, 6, 8) are active** on a drum track; odd macros do nothing. Fixed functions: **Macro 2 = Pitch, Macro 4 = Decay envelope time, Macro 6 = Distortion, Macro 8 = EQ**. Macro LEDs show the drum track's colour. (Note: DJTechTools labels these Pitch/Decay/Drive/Tone — same four functions in reviewer wording.) Drums respond to external MIDI CC on **Channel 10**.

### FX section (delay + reverb)
Press **FX** to open FX View. **16 delay presets and 8 reverb presets**; you can have exactly **one delay preset and one reverb preset active globally** (not different presets per track), but the **send amount is independent per track** and set with the macros — same eight-track macro layout as the Mixer. Delay pads are the "peach" pads on **Rows 1–2 (pads 1–16)**, arranged in increasing complexity from Pad 1→16; all delays are **BPM-synced**, all feature feedback for multiple echoes, some are ping-pong/swung. Delay preset list: 1 Slapback Fast, 2 Slapback Slow, 3 32nd Triplets, 4 32nd, 5 16th Triplets, 6 16th, 7 16th Ping Pong, 8 16th Ping Pong Swung, 9 8th Triplets, 10 8th dotted Ping Pong (stereo spread), 11 8th, 12 8th Ping Pong, 13 8th Ping Pong Swung, 14 4th Triplets, 15 4th dotted Ping Pong Swung, 16 4th Triplets Ping Pong Wide. Reverb pads are the "cream" pads on **Row 3 (pads 17–24)**, increasing reverberation time 17→24. Reverb preset list: 1 Small Chamber, 2 Small Room 1, 3 Small Room 2, 4 Large Room, 5 Hall, 6 Large Hall, 7 Hall – long reflection, 8 Large Hall – long reflection. (The manual's reverb table header is misprinted "DELAY TYPE" but the eight rows are the reverbs.) When you select a reverb preset the macros become reverb send levels (LEDs dim cream); selecting a delay preset makes them delay sends (LEDs peach); the macros adopt whichever function matched the last FX pad pressed. FX sends are automatable (Record + macro; delete with Clear + macro).

### Master Compressor
A **master compressor is applied to the whole mix by default** and is **not adjustable** (no parameters). It is only toggled on/off via the FX button inside **Setup/Advanced Setup View** (p102/106). Reviewers describe it as a "passive master compressor."

### Side-chain
**Side Chain View**: hold **Shift + FX** (or press FX a second time to toggle from FX View). **Seven side-chain presets** per source; **default is OFF** for both synths and both MIDI tracks. Function: the **hits of a chosen drum track duck the audio level** of a synth (or of an external audio input, represented by the MIDI tracks). Page 1 covers Synth 1 / Synth 2; Page 2 covers MIDI 1 / MIDI 2 (the external inputs); switch pages with ▼/▲. In the two lower rows, **Pad 1 = OFF** (bright red when off), **Pads 2–8 = the 7 presets**. **Top-row pads 5–8 choose which Drum track (1–4) is the trigger source** for that track. Creative note: ducking **continues even if the trigger drum's level is set to zero in the Mixer**, but **muting that drum in Mixer View disables side-chain triggering**. Good starting experiment: hold a single synth note with Gate 16 (continuous) and let Drum 1 play a kick — cycle presets to hear the pumping.

### Master Filter knob
The large **Master Filter** knob (top-right of panel) filters **the entire output — all six internal tracks plus both external audio inputs**. It is a single DJ-style knob with a **centre detent**: at centre no filtering occurs and the LED is dimly white. **Turn anticlockwise = low-pass** (removes highs → muffled, drums/low notes remain then disappear). **Turn clockwise = high-pass** (removes lows/bass → thinner sound). LED turns pale blue when either filter is active, brightening as you turn. Always available in every view — a key performance/transition control.

### Mixer view
Press **Mixer** to open Mixer View. **Eight-channel mixer**; default track level is **100** (range 0–127); overall output trimmed by the physical Master Volume knob. **Row 1 pads = Mute buttons** for each of the 8 tracks (mute stops the sequencer triggering that track's notes/hits/MIDI/CC; pad dims when muted). **There is NO solo function** — mute only. By default the macros are **level** controls (▼ button lit; macro LEDs in track colour, dimming as level drops); macros 3 and 4 control the **external Input 1 and Input 2** levels. Press **▼** to switch macros to **PAN** (▼ goes out, ▲ lights): centre = white LED, pan left = increasingly bright blue, pan right = increasingly bright pink; hold **Clear + turn clockwise** to re-centre. Press **▲** to return macros to level. Both level and pan are automatable in Record Mode (delete via Clear + turn). The 16 **Scene** pads live in the lower rows of Mixer View (song-section launching for live performance).

### Audio output routing (rear panel)
- **Outputs L/Mono and R**: two **¼" TS** jack sockets, main audio outs; max output +5.3 dBu. With no plug in R, the L/Mono socket carries a **mono sum** of L+R. (Note: SoS/DJTechTools call these "balanced," but the manual specifies **TS/unbalanced** — trust the manual.)
- **Headphones**: dedicated headphone jack; main Outputs stay active when headphones are plugged in; drives +5 dBu into 150-ohm phones.
- **Inputs 1 and 2**: two **¼" TS unbalanced** line-level audio inputs; mixed with internal sounds, individually treatable by FX, and duckable by the drum tracks (side-chain).
- **Sync**: 3.5 mm TRS analogue clock out, 5 V, default 2 pulses per quarter note.
- **MIDI In / Out / Thru**: full set of three 5-pin DIN; Thru can be reconfigured to clone Out.
- **USB-C**: power/charging + MIDI (class-compliant). **Does NOT carry audio.** So no multitrack/stem USB audio — recording into a DAW must go via the analogue outs.
- **microSD**: for Packs. Built-in rechargeable battery (~4 hrs, reviewer-sourced) enables screen-free untethered play.

### Practical takeaways for a screen-free jam-oriented beginner
- No screen anywhere — everything is pad colour + LED brightness. Filter knob and Mixer/mutes are your main live controls.
- Set key/scale first in Scales View (Major/Minor/Dorian/Mixolydian etc. map cleanly to the guitarist's mental model); it's non-destructive so experiment freely.
- Only one global delay + one global reverb, but per-track sends — plan the "space" of the whole project, not per-instrument reverb.
- Deep sound design requires Components (browser app + USB); on the box you shape sound with the 8 macros only.
- To jam with live musicians: use MIDI DIN or the analogue Sync out to lock to other gear; feed a bass/guitar (line level) into Inputs 1/2 to run it through the filter, FX and side-chain.
