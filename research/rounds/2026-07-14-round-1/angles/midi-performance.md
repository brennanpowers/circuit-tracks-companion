## MIDI & Live Performance — Circuit Tracks field notes

All facts below are drawn from the **Circuit Tracks User Guide V3** and **Circuit Tracks Programmer's Reference Guide V3** (both explicitly Circuit Tracks, product number 100/0x64 in SysEx), plus the Sound on Sound Circuit Tracks review and Novation's own CT support spec pages. Where a fact is inference rather than stated, it's flagged.

### The two MIDI tracks — what they are and do
The two MIDI tracks (labelled MIDI 1 / MIDI 2, blue) "behave just like the Synth tracks and support all the same sequencer functions" but drive **external gear**, not an internal engine. Note data comes from the Note View performance pads and from the 16-step sequencer exactly as on synth tracks. They send on their own MIDI channel via MIDI Out DIN and USB. Clever routing detail: on a MIDI track, the **Macro knobs in Mixer View / FX View act on the two rear-panel audio Inputs**, not on MIDI — the intended workflow is "send MIDI out to your external synth, route that synth's audio back into Circuit Tracks' inputs 1 & 2," so you get level/pan/reverb/delay control over the outboard synth inside CT's own mixer. Macro controls 3 and 4 in Mixer View set the level of audio Inputs 1 and 2 respectively.

### MIDI channels (Setup View)
Factory defaults: Synth 1 = Ch 1, Synth 2 = Ch 2, **MIDI 1 = Ch 3, MIDI 2 = Ch 4**, all four Drums = Ch 10 (they share one channel). **Channel 16 is reserved for the Project** (global reverb/delay/master-filter/mixer CCs and project program-change). Any track can be reassigned to any of channels 1–15; "No two tracks can transmit on the same MIDI channel." Change them in Setup View (hold **Shift + Save**, exit with **Play**): select the track's button, then press the pad for the desired channel on the top two rows. SoS flags a real jam limitation: "there's no focus mode or master channel to save you from fiddling with channels."

### MIDI templates (the eight CC macro presets)
Eight templates are stored inside CT. Enter **Template View by selecting a MIDI track and pressing Preset (button 14)**; the top row of blue pads = the 8 templates; press a pad and it lights white. Each MIDI track can use a **different** template. A template defines what MIDI CC each of the 8 Macro knobs transmits, on that track's channel. The eight defaults are identical, mapping: Macro 1→CC1 (mod wheel), 2→CC2 (breath), 3→CC5 (portamento), 4→CC11 (expression), 5→CC12 (fx ctrl 1), 6→CC13 (fx ctrl 2), 7→CC71 (resonance), 8→CC74 (filter freq). Templates are **edited/created/backed-up only in Novation Components** (web or desktop editor): you set each control's start value (knob fully anticlockwise) and end value (fully clockwise), and mark it unipolar or bipolar — but polarity **only affects the LED behaviour, not the transmitted data**. Novation Components also has downloadable pre-made templates for various synths. Two performance-relevant subtleties: (1) MIDI-template CC automation, once recorded, is sent to **both the MIDI Out DIN and USB**, unlike synth/drum parameter automation which goes to USB only; (2) selecting a new template does not erase existing automation — the recorded knob moves get re-applied to whatever CC the new template assigns to that macro.

### Program Change / patch & project recall over MIDI
PGM on Ch 1 selects Synth 1 patch (0–63), Ch 2 selects Synth 2 patch. **PGM on Ch 16 value 0–63 changes the whole Project instantly; value 64–127 queues the Project change** (waits for a pattern boundary) — useful for clean remote song-section changes from a foot-controller or DAW.

### Clock, tempo, swing, sync
Internal tempo range **40–240 BPM, default 120, integer BPM only**. Set with **Macro 1 in Tempo View** (press Tempo/Swing button 16; LED blue). Slaved to external MIDI clock, CT will follow **any rate 30–300 BPM including fractional tempos**; when locked it shows "SYN" in red and Macro 1 is disabled. **Tap Tempo: hold Shift and tap the Tempo/Swing button** — needs ≥3 taps, then averages the last 5. **Swing = Macro 2 in Tempo View (LED orange), range 20–80, default 50**; it shifts even-numbered (off-beat) steps earlier (low) or later (high). Click/metronome: **Shift + Clear** toggles it (quarter-note tick on all outputs); Macro 5 in Tempo View sets its level.

**Clock settings (Setup View → pads 31/32):** Clock Rx OFF = internal only, all external clock ignored. **Clock Rx ON = AUTO** — CT locks to a valid external MIDI clock at MIDI In *or* USB, and falls back to internal if none is present. **Clock Tx ON = CT is master**, streaming clock out of both USB and MIDI Out. Rx/Tx are independently switchable for four data categories via pads 25–32: Note (green, 25/26), CC (orange, 27/28), Program Change (purple, 29/30), Clock (pale blue, 31/32). All default ON. Caution for jams: if CT is slaved and the external clock **stops or goes out of range, CT stops playing** and holds "SYN" until you hit Play.

**Analogue Sync Out** (rear 3.5mm): 5 V pulse, selectable **1 / 2 / 4 / 8 / 24 ppqn (default 2 ppqn)** via Setup pads 17–21 — this is how you clock non-MIDI analogue/modular gear. Swing is **not** applied to the analogue sync output. There's also a MIDI Thru port that can be reconfigured in **Advanced Setup View (hold Shift while powering on)** via the Duplicate button to act as a second MIDI Out (drive two thru-less synths).

**Ableton Link:** CT has **no network/Wi-Fi hardware and the manuals never mention Link — it does NOT support Ableton Link natively.** Sync to a DAW is via MIDI clock only (CT as master or slave), or via analogue sync. To sit in an Ableton Link session you'd need a bridge (a computer/app translating Link↔MIDI clock).

### Live performance toolkit
**Mute/solo:** Mixer View (button 11). Row-1 pads are per-track mutes; a muted pad dims; press again to unmute. There is **no dedicated Solo** — you perform by muting. Mute is also a play-over tool: when a track is muted "its sequencer step pads become inactive" and become **live performance pads** — you can finger-drum or play the muted synth's assigned notes/chords in real time over the running unmuted tracks. Also note there's **no "Pattern Off" state** (SoS): to silence a track by pattern you keep an empty pattern around.

**Pattern switching & timing:** press a pattern pad. **Default = queued: the current pattern plays to its end, then the new one starts** (queued pad flashes fast) — smooth, in-time. **Hold Shift while selecting = immediate switch that preserves continuity** — CT jumps into the new pattern at the equivalent step (if you were on step 11, new pattern enters at step 12). Selecting two patterns loops them as a pair (basic Pattern Chain; patterns can be 1–32 steps).

**Scenes:** 16 Scene pads live on the Mixer page — each is a snapshot of the currently-playing set of patterns/chains across all tracks. Queuing a scene: its pad flashes green and it swaps in **at the end of the currently-playing Drum 1 pattern, without losing sync**. Chain scenes into whole song arrangements. The Mixer page (scenes + mutes + levels) is described as "the ideal place to coordinate a live performance."

**Master Filter** (the big dedicated knob, control 2): DJ-style filter across the entire output — all six internal tracks plus both external audio inputs. Centre detent = no filtering (LED dim white); anticlockwise = low-pass (bass survives, treble removed); clockwise = high-pass (bass removed, thin). This is the go-to transition/build tool and needs no menu. Over MIDI it's Ch16 CC74 (0–63 low-pass, 64 off, 65–127 high-pass), resonance Ch16 CC71.

**FX as performance:** FX View (button 12) offers **16 delay presets (peach pads, rows 1–2) and 8 reverb presets (cream pads, row 3)**; delay time is BPM-synced (some ping-pong/swung). Per-track send amounts are the Macro knobs. A master compressor is on by default (toggle in Advanced Setup via the FX button).

**Live key/scale change:** Scales View (button 9) — 16 scales and a root note. Both **scale and root note can be changed during playback**. Changing the keynote **transposes the existing sequenced notes non-destructively**; if the new scale lacks a note, CT intelligently substitutes the note ±1 semitone, and reverting restores the original. This is the "change key mid-jam" mechanism a guitarist/bassist will want.

**Clean stop/start:** pressing **Play while running restarts the pattern from Step 1**; hold **Shift + Play to continue from the step where it stopped** — the latter is how you re-enter in time with a live band without jumping the phrase.

### Audio out / PA / amp considerations
Main outputs are **two ¼" TS jacks (L/Mono, R)**, max level **+5.3 dBu (line level)** — go straight into line/DI inputs on a PA mixer. With **nothing plugged into R, L/Mono carries a summed mono mix** — one cable to a mono channel works. Separate headphone out; mains stay live when headphones are inserted. Important: **the USB port does not carry audio** — USB is MIDI/power/firmware only, so at a jam you take audio from the ¼" jacks, not USB. Two ¼" audio inputs (for looping external-synth audio back in) sit at roughly synth/line level. No published round-trip latency figure was found in the reviewed docs; treat any latency claim as unverified.
