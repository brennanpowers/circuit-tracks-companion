FIELD NOTES — SEQUENCING & ARRANGEMENT ON THE NOVATION CIRCUIT TRACKS
All facts below are drawn from the official **Circuit Tracks User Guide, Version 3** (Focusrite/Novation), which is explicitly and exclusively about Circuit Tracks. Page numbers cited. Button index numbers in parentheses match the guide's top-panel diagram (e.g. Record = (13), Velocity/Gate = (6), Pattern Settings = (7), Save = (19), Duplicate = (18), Shift = (20), Clear = (17), Step Page = (8), Patterns = (10), up/down octave = (15)).

DEVICE SHAPE (context): 8 tracks — Synth 1, Synth 2, MIDI 1, MIDI 2, Drum 1–4. Each synth is 6-voice polyphonic (up to 6 notes per step). 32 velocity-sensitive RGB pads. Each Project holds 8 Patterns PER TRACK. 64 internal Project slots; microSD "Packs" (32 packs) extend this.

=== LIVE RECORDING (real-time) ===
Enter Note View for the track (press **Note**, then **Synth 1/Synth 2**, or a Drum track). Notes you play are NOT recorded until you press the **Record** (13) button — you can freely audition against the running pattern first. Press Record once to arm, play, press Record again to stop (p35). The Record button is momentary/latching: a tap latches record on; holding it >~half a second drops out of Record the instant you release — good for punch-in/punch-out (p36). While recording, the step cursor changes from white to RED as a warning you're altering the pattern.
Octave buttons (15) chosen at record time set the octave you play in but have NO effect on pitch during replay. To transpose an existing pattern hold **Shift + Octave** (p36) — but note this is not stored until a manual Save.
DRUMS record differently: in Note View with Record on, just hit the sample pads in real time; the Pattern must be RUNNING (press Play) to hear drum hits (unlike synths) (p64).

=== NON-QUANTISED RECORD (micro steps) ===
Live recording is quantised to the nearest step by default. To enable non-quantised: hold **Shift + Record** (the Record button is also legended **Rec Quantise**) (p37). Confirm state by pressing **Shift**: Record lit GREEN = quantised, RED = non-quantised. Non-quantised notes land on one of SIX "micro steps" (ticks) between adjacent steps; a tick = 1/6 of a step. For drums the same Shift+Record toggle: green when held = quantised, dim red = non-quantised (p64).

=== RECORDING KNOB / MACRO AUTOMATION ===
Enter Record and move any of the 8 Macro knobs (3): the LED under the knob turns RED to confirm it's being recorded (p36). CRUCIAL: you must EXIT Record before the sequence loops, or Circuit Tracks overwrites the automation you just recorded with the new knob position (p37). You can also write automation while STOPPED: in Note View press Record, press-and-HOLD the step pad (plays that step's note), turn the Macro — value written to that step; press Record to exit (p37). While playing you can hold a step pad + turn a Macro to edit that step. CLEARING AUTOMATION: hold **Clear** and move the target knob by at least 20% of its rotation (LED turns red) — this wipes that Macro's automation for the WHOLE Pattern, not just the current step (p37). Drum tracks only record automation in Velocity/Gate/Probability views, and only even-numbered Macros (2,4,6,8) are active for drums (p71). Automation is stored independently of pattern data (p71).

=== STEP EDITING (offline / Stop mode) ===
Note View: top TWO rows = the 16-step pattern display; bottom two rows = the synth keyboard (p38). Steps that contain notes light BRIGHT BLUE. Press-and-hold a step pad to see/hear its notes — the keyboard pad(s) light RED (may be two pads if note is boundary octave); if no keyboard pad lights red the note is in another octave, hunt with Octave buttons (p39).
DELETE a note: press+hold step pad (red), then press the unwanted keyboard pad (p40).
INSERT a note: hold the step pad and press the desired keyboard note — Record does NOT need to be on; you can stack multiple notes on one step (p40).
CLEAR a whole step: hold **Clear** (bright red) and press the step pad — deletes ALL notes at that step in all octaves at once (p40).
DUPLICATE (copy/paste a step): hold **Duplicate** (green), press source step (green; its notes flash red), press destination step (single red blink) — overwrites destination, copies notes plus all attributes. Keep Duplicate held to paste to many steps (p40).

=== PER-STEP VELOCITY ===
Velocity View = press **Velocity** (6). Two lower rows = a 16-pad "fader." Live recording captures 7-bit velocity (0–127); the display resolution is 16 increments of 8. Exact table (No. lit pads → value): 1→8, 2→16, 3→24, 4→32, 5→40, 6→48, 7→56, 8→64, 9→72, 10→80, 11→88, 12→96, 13→104, 14→112, 15→120, 16→127 (p43–44). Change a step's velocity by pressing the corresponding fader pad; do it live by holding the step pad while playing.
FIXED VELOCITY: hold **Shift + Velocity** — sets velocity to a fixed **96** (12 pads). It's a GLOBAL toggle (affects all tracks) and does NOT alter previously recorded notes (p43). Drum hits programmed via step pads always use fixed velocity regardless of mode (p69).
PER-NOTE VELOCITY: notes stacked on one step can hold DIFFERENT velocity values (unless Fixed) — brightly lit pads show the lowest velocity at the step, dim pads the highest (p44).

=== PER-STEP GATE (note length) ===
Gate View = press **Gate** (6). Gate = duration in units of STEPS, ranging from 1/6 up to 16, in increments of 1/6 → 96 possible values (p45). Gate of 16 makes the note sound continuously through an entire 16-step pattern. Set integer gate by pressing that-numbered pad in the fader. FRACTIONAL gate: press the highest lit pad AGAIN — each additional press subtracts 1/6 step (pad dims incrementally); after the 5th press the 6th press reverts to the original integer value (p46). Steps with no notes have zero gate and can't be edited.

=== PROBABILITY ===
Probability View is the SECONDARY view of **Pattern Settings** (7): open by holding **Shift + Pattern Settings**, or press Pattern Settings a second time when already in Pattern Settings View (p47). Pads 17–24 = an 8-level "probability meter." EIGHT values (lit pads → chance): 1–8→100%, 1–7→87.5%, 1–6→75%, 1–5→62.5%, 1–4→50%, 1–3→37.5%, 1–2→25%, 1 only→12.5% (p47). Probability is per-STEP (all notes/hits at a step trigger together or not at all). Assign in Stop mode: press the step, then a Row-3 pad. Clearing steps/patterns/projects resets probability to 100%; live-recording a new note to a step also resets that step to 100% (p48).

=== MICRO STEP EDIT / NUDGE ===
Micro Step View is the SECONDARY view of **Gate** (6): hold **Shift + Gate**, or press Gate twice (p48). Six micro-nudge values shown in the third grid row (labelled 0–5); pad "0" lit = on the beat (default, zero delay). Row 4 (pads 25+) shows the individual NOTES present at the step (up to 6, left-to-right in entry order); select a note (auditions it), then press a micro-step pad 0–5 to delay it by that many ticks (1 tick = 1/6 step) (p49). Enables triplets across the beat and "strummed" chords (staggered playback). For DRUMS, micro steps are set in Gate View, pads 17–22 (six), and each of the 6 micro steps can be toggled on/off so one step can retrigger a sample multiple times per step (p66–67). All drum micro-hits inherit the major step's velocity and sample (p68).

=== TIED / DRONE NOTES ===
Access via Gate View → select the step containing the note to tie → switch to Micro Step View → press **pad 24** to toggle "tie-forward" on/off (p51–52). Set the gate long enough that the note ends just before/overlaps the next. A tie on step 1 with gate = 16 plays indefinitely if that pattern is the only one playing; otherwise create a matching-pitch note at the start of the next pattern in the chain to tie them (p52). In Note View, tied notes appear ORANGE (not red) when the step is held. Newly added notes on a tied step do NOT inherit the tie (shown red) — this prevents multiple ties from overdubbing.

=== PATTERN LENGTH: 16 / 32 / SHORTER ===
Default pattern length = 16 steps; double to 32 with the **Step Page** button (8), legended **1-16/17-32**: blue = Page 1 (steps 1–16), orange = Page 2 (steps 17–32) (p75). A pattern can also be made SHORTER than 16, or any length up to a max of 32, via Pattern Settings start/end points (p53). Hold **Clear + Step Page** reverts to 16 steps (steps 17–32 data preserved). Hold **Duplicate + Step Page** extends to 32 and copies steps 1–16 into 17–32 (incl. automation), overwriting any page-2 data (p75).
START/END POINTS (Pattern Settings View, upper two rows): default end = pad 16 (lit sand). Press a different step pad to move the END point (shortens pattern); higher pads that still hold data glow dim red. Move the START point by holding **Shift** while pressing the new start pad (p53–54). Different tracks can independently have different lengths (a 16-step track repeats twice against a 32-step track) (p75).
PLAY ORDER (Pattern Settings, pads 29–32): 29 = Forwards (default), 30 = Reverse, 31 = Ping-pong, 32 = Random (p55). Changing it in Play mode completes the current cycle first.
PATTERN SYNC RATE (Pattern Settings, third row pads 1–8): a per-track multiplier/divider of project BPM. Values: 1/4, 1/4T, 1/8, 1/8T, **1/16 (default, pad 5)**, 1/16T, 1/32, 1/32T (T = triplet) (p55). Higher = finer step resolution but shorter loop; lower = longer evolving patterns. Changes at end of current cycle in Play mode.

=== MUTATE ===
Hold **Shift + Duplicate** to Mutate (p56). Works in any view with a pattern-step display (Note/Velocity/Gate/Pattern Settings). It SHUFFLES the existing notes/hits to different steps — count of notes and the actual sounds are unchanged, but all step parameters (micro steps, gate, sample flips, probability, automation) are reassigned. Only affects the currently PLAYING pattern (chained patterns unaffected). Repeat Shift+Duplicate to re-mutate. CANNOT be undone — save the Project first.

=== PATTERNS & CHAINING ===
8 Patterns per track. Patterns View = **Patterns** (10). Two pages via up/down (15): Page 1 = Patterns 1–4, Page 2 = 5–8, arranged vertically; 8 columns = the 8 tracks (p73). Switch pattern by pressing its pad (Stop or Play). In Play, selecting another pattern QUEUES it (plays current to end; queued pad flashes) — OR hold **Shift** while selecting to switch immediately, continuing from the corresponding step for seamless timing (p74). Play restarts from Step 1 / start point; **Shift + Play** continues from where stopped.
CLEAR a pattern: hold **Clear** + pattern pad. DUPLICATE a pattern: hold **Duplicate**, press source, press destination — you can copy synth↔synth/MIDI and drum↔drum, but NOT between synth/MIDI and drum tracks (p75).
CHAIN patterns: hold the LOWEST pattern pad then press the HIGHEST (e.g. hold pad 1, press pad 3 = chain 1-2-3). Must be CONTIGUOUS/consecutive — you can chain 1-2-3-4 or 5-6-7, not 1-2-6 (p76). Cross-page chains work by holding start pad, pressing down to Page 2, then the end pad. A chain of up to 8 patterns; with all 32-step patterns that's up to **256 steps (8 × 32)** (p78). Each track loops its OWN chain independently; the longest chain defines overall sequence length. **Pattern Octave**: hold **Shift + up/down** (15) to transpose an entire synth/MIDI pattern (p79). **View Lock**: hold **Shift + Patterns** to freeze the step display on one pattern while another plays (Patterns button green=locked when Shift held; not saved, resets on power-on) (p80).

=== SCENES (song arrangement) ===
16 Scenes, in **Mixer View** (press **Mixer**). The two lower rows = the 16 scene pads (pads 17–32); Scene 1 default (pad 17 pulsing green) (p81). A Scene stores a snapshot of the pattern/pattern-chain selection for ALL tracks — letting you trigger a whole song section from one pad, and OVERCOME the contiguous-pattern chaining restriction.
ASSIGN: first define the desired pattern chains per track in Patterns View → go to Mixer View → hold **Shift** (scene pads turn dim gold) → press a scene pad (bright gold) → release Shift (pad turns bright white = saved) (p81–82). Scene data is stored with the Project only when you Save (p83).
QUEUE scenes: like patterns — a queued scene flashes green and starts at the end of the currently-playing Drum 1 pattern without losing sync (p84).
CHAIN scenes: hold the first scene pad, press the last — those pads and all between light green; scenes play in numerical order then repeat (p83). This is how you build a full song from sections. CLEAR scene: hold **Clear** + scene pad (resets to Pattern 1 all tracks). DUPLICATE scene: hold **Duplicate**, source, destination (p84).

=== PROJECTS & SAVE ===
64 internal Project memories (p5). SAVE = press **Save** (19) TWICE: first press flashes the button, second press writes to the LAST-USED slot (overwrites the original) (p96, p22). To save to a NEW slot: go to Projects View, first Save press pulses the current slot white, then press the target pad — it flashes green briefly. Abort after first press by pressing any other button. Nothing is auto-saved — octave shifts, mutate, edits etc. are lost on power-off unless manually saved. Project colour can be set during Save by turning Macro 1 (14 colours) (p96). A **Save Lock** feature (appendix) can disable saving entirely.
SWITCHING projects live: in Play, pressing a project pad QUEUES it (current PATTERN only — not scene/chain — plays to end, new pad flashes white); holding **Shift** + selecting switches immediately from the same step in the chain (p95). Tempo of new project replaces the old.

MIDI TRACKS: behave like synth tracks for all sequencer functions (notes, per-step params, automation) but send to external gear; 8 selectable MIDI templates, Macros default to CC 1,2,5,11,12,13,71,74 (p57–58). Automated MIDI template controls send to the DIN MIDI Out as well as USB.

PITFALLS worth flagging for a beginner: (1) Mutate is destructive with no undo. (2) Recording knob automation requires exiting Record before the loop wraps or it's overwritten. (3) Save overwrites the source project by default — use Projects View to pick a new slot. (4) Drum notes are silent in Note View when the pattern is stopped (use Velocity/Gate view to audition). (5) Clearing a step wipes velocity/gate/micro/probability too.
