You have a groove, a bassline, chords, space, and movement — all one bar long. This
lesson is where a loop becomes a *song*: intro, build, drop, breakdown, out.

## Longer patterns

<PanelHighlight controls={['step-page', 'patterns', 'mixer']} caption="Step Page doubles the bar, Patterns holds your variants, and Scenes live in Mixer." />

1. Your bassline wants room to evolve. On `Synth 1`, press `Step Page` (the
   1-16/17-32 button): the pattern doubles to **32 steps**. Better: hold `Duplicate`
   + press `Step Page` — it extends AND copies steps 1–16 into 17–32 (automation
   included), so you edit the second half into a variation instead of starting empty.
2. While playing a 32-step pattern, the `Step Page` legend tells you which half
   you're looking at (blue = 1–16, orange = 17–32).
3. Patterns don't have to be 16 or 32 — **Pattern Settings** lets you drag start and
   end points anywhere ([odd lengths = polymeter fuel](07-patterns-scenes-arrangement.md)).

## More patterns, chained

4. Each track has **8 pattern slots** (`Patterns` view, `▼`/`▲` for slots 5–8). Build
   variants: Pattern 1 = main groove, 2 = variation (your Mutate keeper), 3 =
   breakdown (drums thinned), 4 = fill.
5. Chain them: hold the **lowest** pad, press the **highest** — patterns 1–4 now loop
   in order. Chains must be contiguous (1-2-3-4, never 1-2-4) — Scenes fix that next.
6. Tracks chain independently: drums can cycle 4 patterns while the bass loops one.
   Longest chain sets the cycle; everyone else repeats to fill. (Instant polymeter if
   the lengths disagree on purpose.)

## Scenes: the arrangement layer

A **Scene** snapshots *which patterns/chains every track is playing* — and, on your
firmware (1.2), the **mute states** too. 16 of them live on the bottom two rows of
Mixer View.

7. Set the board how the *intro* should sound (mute synths, drums on pattern 1). In
   `Mixer`, hold `Shift` — scene pads glow dim gold — and tap **scene pad 1**
   (bright gold). Intro captured.
8. Repeat for your sections: 2 = groove + bass, 3 = full (chords in), 4 = breakdown,
   5 = variation/fill, 6 = outro.

<PanelHighlight pads={['17:gold', '18:gold', '19:gold', '20:gold', '21:gold', '22:gold']} numbered caption="Six scenes for the arrangement — pad 17 (intro) through pad 22 (outro), on Mixer View's scene rows." /> Tap scene pads while playing: switches are
   **queued to the end of Drum 1's pattern**, so they land musically. `Shift` + pad
   jumps immediately.
9. Chain the song: hold scene 1, press scene 6 — the whole arrangement plays itself
   in order and repeats. You've built song mode.

<TheoryBridge>
  An arrangement is an energy curve, not a sequence of sections. Intro-build-drop-
  breakdown-out is just one shape of the same underlying move: tension accumulates
  (layers stack, the filter opens, the register climbs) and then releases (a drop, a
  strip-back, a silence). When you sketch scenes, sketch the curve first — "where is
  the peak, and what am I withholding until it?" — and let the sections fall out of
  that. Every structure you'll ever build on this device is some shape of that curve.
</TheoryBridge>

## Ship it

10. `Save` twice (or save-as to a new slot, coloured with whatever system you
    started back in First Groove). Scenes and chains persist **only** in a saved
    project.

<JamTip>
  Queued scene switching is your no-stress live mode: tap the next section any time
  during the current one, and the device handles the transition on the boundary.
</JamTip>

A rule for closing projects, this one and every one after it: **a track is done when
each scene earns its place on the energy curve — not when you run out of ideas to
add.** If a new layer doesn't change the curve, it's clutter; if a section neither
builds nor releases, cut it. Save, name it by colour, stop. Finished beats perfect,
every time.
