## The keyboard that's always in key

1. Press `Synth 1` (violet). Note View again — but now the top two rows are the
   synth's 16 steps and the **bottom two rows are a keyboard**. Out of the box it's
   C Natural Minor: every pad is in-key, wrong notes are literally unreachable.
2. The two rows overlap by one note: the **last** pad of the lower keyboard row
   (pad 32, bottom-right corner of the grid) is the **same pitch** as the **first**
   pad of the row above it (pad 17, that row's left end). A two-octave run goes:
   bottom row left-to-right, then the row above from its *second* pad onward.

3. `▼`/`▲` shift octaves (up to 5 up / 6 down, per track). Go down until it growls.

## Choose your key like a musician

<PanelHighlight controls={['synth-1', 'scales', 'arrows']} caption="Synth 1, the Scales view, and the octave arrows — your key-and-register controls." />

4. Press `Scales`. Bottom two rows = **16 scales**; top rows = the **root note** on a
   C-to-B layout (white notes along pads 9–16, black notes above). Root and scale
   apply to *both* synths — one global key per project.

<PanelHighlight pads={['9:white', '10:white', '11:white', '12:white', '13:white', '14:white', '15:white', '16:white']} numbered caption="Scales View, root-note row: pads 9–16 are the white notes C to B — tap one to set the project's root." />
5. You know these names: Dorian, Mixolydian, Phrygian… pick what the track wants —
   Natural Minor or Dorian are money for house. Changing scale later is
   **non-destructive**: out-of-scale notes get substituted and *revert* if you switch
   back, so audition freely, even during playback.

One escape hatch to know about: the 16th scale, **Chromatic** (pad 32 in Scales
View), removes the note filter entirely — all 12 notes, laid out piano-style, one
octave per screen. It's how you play a deliberate passing tone or an outside note
once always-in-key starts feeling like a fence. File it away; today the fence helps.

<TheoryBridge>
  For fretboard thinkers: this is your neck with the "wrong" notes removed. In a 7-note scale, one
  row spans an octave: root on the left, the 5th four pads over, the octave at the
  row's end + the overlap pad. Think scale degrees, not note names — the shapes are
  position-invariant, just like fret patterns, and they stay identical in every key.
</TheoryBridge>

## Write the line

6. Two ways, same as drums. **Step entry**: hold a step pad, press a keyboard pad —
   that note lands on that step, no Record needed (this is the precision tool).
   **Live record**: press `Record` and play; punch out before the loop repeats if
   you're also touching knobs.
7. Classic starting point: root notes on the kick (steps 1, 5, 9, 13), then replace
   step 13's root with the 5th or the octave. Leave space — steps without notes are
   groove too.
8. Wrong note? Hold the step pad and press the offending keyboard pad to delete just
   that note; `Clear` + step wipes the step.
