Synth 2 (pale green) is your harmony department: six-note polyphonic, loaded with a
poly patch by default, and sharing the key you already set — so every chord you can
grab is in the right scale.

## Chords, the step-entry way

<PanelHighlight controls={['synth-2', 'gate', 'note']} caption="Synth 2 holds the chords; Gate shapes stabs vs pads; Shift + Note opens the four-octave keyboard." />

1. `Synth 2`, pick a comfortable octave. Hold a **step pad** and press **several
   keyboard pads at once** (or one after another while holding): they stack, up to
   **six notes per step**.
2. Build a progression across the bar: chord on step 1, another on step 5, 9, 13.
   In-scale triads are every-other-note shapes **within a keyboard row**: counting
   from the row's left edge, hold its 1st, 3rd, and 5th pads — in the upper keyboard
   row that's pads 17, 19, 21. Slide the same three-pad shape along the row for the
   other triads.

<PanelHighlight pads={['17:green', '19:green', '21:green']} numbered caption="A root triad on the upper keyboard row: pads 17, 19, 21 — then slide the shape right for the next chord." />
3. Each note in the stack keeps its own velocity — the keyboard pads are
   velocity-sensitive during step entry too, so pressing a pad more gently enters a
   softer note. Voice your chords by entering the top note softer.

## Stabs vs pads: it's all gate

4. Press `Gate`, select a chord step. Gate = how long the notes hold, from 1/6 of a
   step to 16 full steps. Short (1–2) = house stabs; long = washy pads.
5. For a sustained pad under the whole bar: one chord on step 1, gate 16. For
   drones that never re-trigger, there's tie-forward — pad 24 in Micro Step View —
   which you'll use heavily in Project 4 ([details](06-sequencing.md)).
6. Strum trick from your guitar life: in Micro Step View, nudge the chord's notes to
   *different* micro positions — the stack rakes like a pick across strings instead
   of hitting as a block.

## Record it like an instrument

7. `Shift + Note` — **Expanded Note View**: the step display disappears and you get a
   **four-octave keyboard** (the `Note` button glows gold). This is the intended way
   to record keys live.
8. `Record`, play a pass. Recording captures up to the same six voices per step;
   quantise applies unless you toggled it off. Press `Note` to drop back to the
   normal split view and tidy any step by hand.

<TheoryBridge>
  Six voices is a budget — spend it like an arranger. Root-position triads everywhere
  waste voices an octave below your bass track's register. Drop the chord root (the
  bass already owns it), keep 3rd + 7th + a colour tone, and your six voices cover
  richer harmony with less mud.
</TheoryBridge>

<Checkpoint>
  Enter a four-chord progression as stacked steps; shape one chord into a stab and
  another into a bar-long pad with gate; record a live pass in Expanded Note View.
</Checkpoint>
