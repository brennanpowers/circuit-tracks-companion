Everything so far is dry and flat. This lesson adds depth (reverb), echo (delay), and
the heartbeat compression-feel (side-chain) that makes electronic music breathe.

## One reverb, one delay, everyone shares

<PanelHighlight controls={['fx', 'shift']} caption="FX for sends; hold Shift with it for Side Chain." />

The FX architecture in one sentence: there's a single global **reverb** (8 presets)
and a single global **delay** (16 presets, all tempo-synced), and every track has its
own **send level** into each.

1. Press `FX`. Rows 1–2 (peach pads) are the delay presets, row 3 (cream) the
   reverbs. Tap to choose — one of each, project-wide.
2. Sensible starting picks: a mid-sized room reverb, and an **8th- or dotted-8th
   ping-pong** delay (they're all locked to your BPM, so everything echoes in time).
3. Now the sends: after tapping a reverb pad, the 8 macros set **per-track reverb
   send** (LEDs glow cream). Tap a delay pad and the same knobs become **delay
   sends** (peach). Send the snare into the reverb, a synth stab into the delay,
   and keep the kick and bass nearly dry — low end + reverb = mud.

## Balance and width — the mixer's other half

Depth handled, two more dimensions. Everything so far plays at factory levels, and a
mix where every track is equally loud is a mix where nothing leads.

1. Press `Mixer`. The eight macros are now the eight tracks' **levels** — knob 1 =
   Synth 1, knob 2 = Synth 2, knobs 5–8 = Drums 1–4 (0–127, everything starts
   at 100). Pull the chord track down until it sits *behind* the bass; nudge the
   kick up. Small moves — 10 or 15 — go a long way.
2. Press `▼` and the same knobs become **pan** (white LED = centre, blue = left,
   pink = right). Push the hats a touch right and the texture perc a touch left —
   the mix suddenly has *width*. Keep kick, snare, and bass centred (low end lives
   in the middle). `▲` puts the knobs back on levels.
3. Both moves record as automation in Record mode, same rules as every other knob.

<TheoryBridge>
  This is ordinary band-mix instinct: players don't compete on volume, they occupy
  different pockets. Level = who's in front; pan = who's standing where on the
  stage.
</TheoryBridge>

## The pump

4. `Shift + FX` — **Side Chain View**. Page 1 is the synths (`▼`/`▲` switch pages).
   Each synth row: pad 1 = off (default), pads 2–8 = seven ducking shapes.
5. The trigger — the "key" — is a drum track you pick (top-row pads). Choose
   **Drum 1**, your four-on-the-floor kick.
6. Demo it properly: give Synth 2 a bar-long pad chord (gate 16), then step through
   the seven presets while it plays. The kick now pushes the pad out of its way —
   that rhythmic breathing is *the* house/techno glue.
7. Fine print worth knowing: the duck keeps triggering even if you pull the key
   drum's mixer level to zero (silent pumping — an actual technique), but **muting**
   the key drum stops the ducking too.

<JamTip>
  Live, the delay send is a one-knob dub machine: grab a synth's send macro, crank it
  for one phrase, pull it back. The tail rides the tempo, so it always lands in time.
</JamTip>
