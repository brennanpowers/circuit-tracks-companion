Your bassline is playing the factory default patch. There are 127 others, and the
knob layer above them. This lesson is about *choosing* sounds instead of settling.

## Patch View

<PanelHighlight controls={['preset', 'macro-3', 'macro-5']} caption="Preset opens Patch View; Macros 3 and 5 are the shape-and-brightness pair worth learning first." />

1. With `Synth 1` selected, press `Preset`. The grid becomes 32 patch pads —
   **page 1 of 4** (128 patches total). `▼`/`▲` turn pages.
2. Tapping a patch **auditions it** in your current key (Patch Preview). For silent
   browsing mid-performance, hold `Shift` while tapping — selects without preview.
3. Synth 1's default is Patch 1: monophonic, bass-duty. Factory projects load
   Synth 2 with Patch 33 — a polyphonic keys sound. That split (mono bass / poly
   keys) is convention, not law.

<DeviceLab issue="synth2-preset-page">
  A real error in Novation's manual, and you can be the tiebreaker. The manual claims
  Preset View "opens at page 3" for Synth 2 — but patch 33 arithmetically lives on
  **page 2** (pages hold 32 each). Press `Synth 2`, then `Preset`: which page are you
  on (count `▼` presses back to page 1)? Find patch 33 — page 2, pad 1, correct?
</DeviceLab>

## The macro layer

4. Back on your bass (press `Synth 1`, then `Note`): the 8 macros now shape the
   patch. The panel legend gives the *typical* mapping — 1 Oscillator, 2 Osc Mod,
   3 Amp Envelope, 4 Filter Envelope, 5 Filter Frequency, 6 Resonance, 7 Modulation,
   8 FX — but each patch can reassign up to 4 parameters per knob, so **ears over
   labels**.
5. The two that almost always matter: **Macro 5** (filter frequency — brightness) and
   **Macro 3** (amp envelope — pluck vs swell). Sculpt your bass: darker, snappier.
6. These moves are performance by default and **automation when recorded** — same
   rules you learned on drums: red LED while writing, punch out before the loop,
   `Clear` + knob ≥20% to erase.

Deep sound design — building patches from raw oscillators — happens in Components'
Synth Editor, not on the hardware. That's Project 4 territory. Today is: find a great
factory patch, make it yours with two knobs.

<JamTip>
  Memorize your bass patch's *page and pad position*. On a screenless device, "my
  bass is page 1, pad 7" is the difference between a 2-second and a 2-minute patch
  change under stage lights.
</JamTip>

<Checkpoint>
  Page through all four patch pages and back; silent-select a patch with `Shift`;
  name (by function) what Macros 3 and 5 did to your bass sound.
</Checkpoint>
