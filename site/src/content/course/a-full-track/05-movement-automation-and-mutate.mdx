A static loop, however good, reads as a demo. Movement — parameters that *go
somewhere* over the bar — reads as music. You already know the mechanics from the
drum lessons; now apply them where they shine, and meet the chaos button.

## Automation rides

1. Bass filter sweep: `Synth 1`, pattern playing, `Record` on, ride **Macro 5** up
   over the bar. Punch out **before the loop wraps** (the rule from Project 1 — the
   pass overwrites itself with the knob's resting position if you stay in Record).
2. FX-send automation works identically: in FX View, record the snare's reverb send
   blooming open on beat 4. Sends, mixer levels, pan — all of it records.
3. Surgical version, no playback needed: while stopped in Note View, press `Record`,
   **hold the step pad**, and turn the macro — the value writes to that exact step.
   Precision beats performance when one step needs one value.
4. Erase per-knob as always: hold `Clear`, turn the knob ≥20%.

## Mutate: rolling the dice you loaded

**`Shift + Duplicate` = Mutate.** It takes the notes/hits already in the *currently
playing pattern* and reshuffles them onto different steps — dragging their gates,
velocities, micro steps, probabilities, sample flips, and automation along. Same
ingredients, new recipe. Press again: another roll.

5. First, the discipline: **duplicate before you gamble.** In Patterns View, hold
   `Duplicate`, tap your pattern, tap an empty slot — checkpoint made. Mutate cannot
   be undone (nothing here can).
6. Now mutate your *bassline copy* a few times. Most rolls are garbage, some are
   grooves you'd never write on purpose. Keep a winner; it becomes your variation/
   fill pattern for the arrangement lesson.
7. Mutate respects the scale — synth notes land in-key — and only touches the
   pattern that's playing on the current track. Your other patterns are safe.

<TheoryBridge>
  Mutate is the sequencer version of "play the same notes, different order" — a jazz
  practice-room trick. The harmonic material can't leave the key you set, so what
  you're really auditioning is *rhythmic permutations* of a pitch set you already
  approved.
</TheoryBridge>
