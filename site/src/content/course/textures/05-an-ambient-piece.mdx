Last lesson. No new features — the whole device is now in your hands. What remains is
the thing features can't do: deciding what the piece *is*, and finishing it.

## The assembly

1. **Foundation:** your custom pad patch (lesson 2) on Synth 2, tie-forward drone or
   4-bar sync-rate progression. This is the piece's gravity.
2. **Voice:** Synth 1 carries the generative melody system from lesson 4 — your
   choice of cell, register high enough to sit above the drone.
3. **Ground:** one drum track as the texture collage (your samples, flips,
   probability); another as *almost-rhythm* — a soft pulse at 50% probability, or a
   single sub-kick pitched down, once a bar. Ambient still breathes better with a
   faint pulse to push against.
4. **Space:** a long hall reverb, generous sends on melody and textures, drone
   mostly dry (mud rule). Side-chain, gently: let the soft pulse duck the pad by a
   preset or two — subliminal breathing.

## Shape it in time

5. Scenes, one final time — not song sections now, but *states of weather*: opening
   stillness → first motion → full bloom → recession → close. Five scenes, queued by
   hand as you perform the piece; this music wants human pacing, not a chain.
6. Automation for the long arc: record a filter-open ride across the bloom scene's
   pattern; record the melody's reverb send climbing as it recedes.

## Finish it

7. Perform it once, start to end, phone recording — imperfections included. Then
   `Save` (twice), colour it blue (Textures convention), and **stop editing**.
   A finished four-minute piece teaches more than an immortal work-in-progress.

<JamTip>
