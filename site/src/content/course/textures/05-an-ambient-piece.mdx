Last lesson. No new features — the whole device is now in your hands. What remains is
the thing features can't do: deciding what the piece *is*, and finishing it.

## The assembly

1. **Foundation:** your custom pad patch (lesson 2) on Synth 2, tie-forward drone or
   4-bar sync-rate progression. This is the piece's gravity.
2. **Voice:** Synth 1 carries the generative melody system from lesson 4 — your
   choice of cell, register high enough to sit above the drone.
3. **Ground:** one drum track as the texture collage (your samples, flips,
   probability); another as *almost-rhythm* — a soft pulse at 50% probability, or a
   single sub-kick pitched down, once a bar. Ambient still breathes better with a
   faint pulse to push against.
4. **Space:** a long hall reverb, generous sends on melody and textures, drone
   mostly dry (mud rule). Side-chain, gently: in `Shift + FX`, give Synth 2 one of
   the *lower* ducking presets (its row's pads 2–3; pad 1 is off) with the soft
   pulse's drum as the key — the pad breathes almost subliminally.

## Shape it in time

5. Scenes, one final time — not song sections now, but *states of weather*: opening
   stillness → first motion → full bloom → recession → close. Five scenes, queued by
   hand as you perform the piece; this music wants human pacing, not a chain.

6. Automation for the long arc: record a filter-open ride across the bloom scene's
   pattern; record the melody's reverb send climbing as it recedes.

## Finish it

7. Perform it once, start to end, phone recording — imperfections included. Then
   `Save` (twice), colour it with your system's finished-piece colour, and **stop editing**.
   A finished four-minute piece teaches more than an immortal work-in-progress.

## The real final exam

Everything above still handed you a role list. The last rep removes it: **start an
empty project with no instructions from us.** Pick one way in, set one constraint
(a key, a tempo, or a single sample), and build until it's finished and saved.
That's the whole assignment.

Ways in — you already know all of them, they just never had names:

1. **Remix a demo** — load one, strip it with mutes, rebuild it your way (Start Here).
2. **Drums first** — groove, then bass against the kick, then harmony (First Groove
   → A Full Track: the classic path).
3. **Chords or drone first** — a pad progression as gravity, rhythm later (this
   project's path).
4. **Sample first** — one imported texture on a drum track, pitched and flipped
   until it suggests a piece (lesson 3).
5. **Dice first** — duplicate any old pattern, Mutate until something surprises you,
   keep it and build around it (lesson 4).

Any of these breaks a blank page. When your track sounds wrong along the way, the
cheat sheet's "when it sounds wrong" table is the fix-it map.
