The drum tracks don't care that their samples are drums. Field recordings, vocal
snippets, a plucked bass harmonic, vinyl crackle — anything under the memory budget
becomes playable, flippable, pitchable material. This is where the *Textures* palette
gets personal. (Remember: there's **no sampling on the device itself** — everything
arrives through Components.)

## The rules of the import

1. In Components, open the sample area for your pack. Drag audio in — anything
   reasonable (WAV/AIFF etc.); Components converts on import.
2. Know what conversion means: everything becomes **48 kHz, 16-bit, MONO**. Stereo
   files get down-mixed — your wide field recording will fold to the centre.
   (Width can come back later via the reverb send or a ping-pong delay.)
3. The budget: **196.6 seconds total per pack**, no single sample over 98.3 s.
   Texture loops eat this fast — trim in an editor first; the device isn't where you
   edit audio.
4. Back up the factory pack before overwriting slots (you did this in Setup Day —
   this is why).

## Curate like a kit

5. Slots have kit *meaning* on the device (kicks 1–2, snares 3–4, closed hats 5–6,
   open hats 7–8, perc 9–12, melodic 13–16 per page) — the defaults for each drum
   track point at slots 1/3/5/9. Put your "anchor" sounds there and textures in the
   perc/melodic slots, and new projects will boot straight into your palette.
6. Load a few immediately useful ones: a long airy texture (for Macro-2 pitch-down
   drones), something metallic (for probability sprinkles), one vocal breath.

## Play them wrong on purpose

7. Back on the device: your texture on Drum 4, **Macro 2** pitched way down, decay
   (Macro 4) long — a granular-adjacent bed from one mono file.
8. Sample-flip several textures across one track's steps; give them 25–50%
   probability each. That's a self-shuffling collage — the heart of the final piece.

<JamTip>
  A 96-second ambient bed sample is legal (under the 98.3 s cap) — one pad press =
  an entire atmosphere under the whole jam. Deep sample-time budgeting exists so you
  can make exactly this trade.
</JamTip>
