Every tool in this lesson you already know. The lesson is the *combination*: setting
up a system whose output surprises you, then curating the surprises. That's what
"generative" means in practice — dice plus taste.

## Layer the dice

1. **Probability layers:** on your texture drum track, anchors at 100%, decorations
   at 37.5–50% (Project 1 skills, ambient dosage). On Synth 1's melody: drop *most*
   steps below 75%. Sparse + probabilistic = melodies that feel improvised.
2. **Polymeter:** melody 13 steps, textures 16, drone 4-bar sync rate (lesson 1
   setup). Nothing repeats exactly for a long time.
3. **Play order:** melody to Random or Ping-pong. Random + a 5-note in-scale cell +
   probability = wind-chime logic — never wrong, never the same.

## Mutate as a composing partner

4. Duplicate your melody pattern to slots 2 and 3 (discipline!). Mutate slot 2 a few
   times, keep the best roll; mutate slot 3 into something busier.
5. Chain patterns 1–3: the melody now cycles composed → variation → busy, and with
   the probability dice on top, each cycle differs. You composed 16 steps; the
   system is performing minutes.

## Steering wheel, not autopilot

<PanelHighlight controls={['mixer', 'master-filter', 'fx', 'scales']} caption="While the dice run, you steer with mutes, the Master Filter, FX sends, and Scales." />

6. Your live controls over the system: mutes (bring layers in/out), the Master
   Filter (the "weather"), FX sends (push a layer into the distance), and root/scale
   moves from `Scales` — a root change re-colours everything the dice are doing.
7. Sit with it for ten minutes. Intervene *less* than feels natural. When something
   beautiful happens twice, decide whether to promote it: live-recording a note onto
   a step resets that step's probability to 100% — the mechanism for freezing a
   happy accident into the composition.

<TheoryBridge>
  This is figured bass logic: you define the harmonic frame (scale, cell, registers)
  and the "player" (the dice) realizes it differently each pass. Composition becomes
  choosing constraints — the most bass-player-brained idea in electronic music.
</TheoryBridge>

<Checkpoint>
  A three-layer system (drone / melody / texture) that runs unattended for five
  minutes without exact repetition — and you can name which of the four tools is
  creating each kind of variation you hear.
</Checkpoint>
