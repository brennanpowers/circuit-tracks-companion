Time to make your own noise. Drums first, because rhythm needs zero melody theory and
the drum grid teaches the sequencer's core idea: **a pattern is 16 steps, and each
step either fires or doesn't.**

## An empty canvas

1. `Projects`, pick an **unlit** pad (dark = empty; the lit ones are the demos, as
   you saw in Start Here) — a blank project loads silently at 120 BPM.
2. Press `Drum 1` (orange). You're in its Note View: **top two rows = 16 steps**,
   lower pads = the sample bank. Drum 1 comes loaded with Kick 1.
3. Press `Play`. The white cursor sweeps the 16 steps. Silence — nothing's placed yet.

## Program the backbone

<PanelHighlight controls={['drums', 'grid']} caption="The four drum track buttons select; the grid's top rows are your 16 steps." />

4. **Kick:** tap steps 1, 5, 9, 13 — they light bright blue, and there's your
   four-on-the-floor. Steps read like text: 1–8 across the top row left-to-right,
   9–16 continuing across the row below. (And 16 steps = one bar of 16ths, so every
   4th step is a quarter-note beat.)

<PanelHighlight pads={['1:blue', '5:blue', '9:blue', '13:blue']} numbered caption="The kick on Drum 1: steps 1, 5, 9, 13 lit bright blue — the top two rows are your 16 steps." />

5. **Snare:** press `Drum 2` (yellow), tap steps 5 and 13 — backbeats 2 and 4.
6. **Hats:** press `Drum 3` (purple), tap every odd step (1, 3, 5, …) for straight
   8ths. Charmless — we fix that over the next two lessons.

<PanelHighlight pads={['1:blue', '3:blue', '5:blue', '7:blue', '9:blue', '11:blue', '13:blue', '15:blue']} numbered caption="The hats on Drum 3: every odd step — straight 8th notes." />

To remove a hit, tap its step again. To try different samples: tap a different pad in
the sample bank (a quick tap changes the track's active sample; a **long press just
auditions** without switching). The default bank layout is a kit — kicks in slots 1–2,
snares 3–4, closed hats 5–6, open hats 7–8 ([full map](04-drum-tracks.md)).

## Now do it live

Programming steps is precise; performing is faster and more fun:

7. Hold `Shift` and press `Note` — **Expanded Drum View**. Almost everything goes
   dark except four pads (29–32): live triggers for Drums 1–4, one per finger.

<PanelHighlight pads={[{n: 29, color: 'orange', label: 'D1'}, {n: 30, color: 'gold', label: 'D2'}, {n: 31, color: 'violet', label: 'D3'}, {n: 32, color: 'blue', label: 'D4'}]} numbered caption="Expanded Drum View: everything goes dark except pads 29–32 — one live trigger per drum track." />
8. Press `Record` (lights red) with the pattern playing, and drum a variation on
   those four pads. Your hits land in the pattern, quantised to the nearest step.
9. Press `Record` again to stop recording. Don't like a step it captured? Tap it off
   in Note View, or hold `Clear` and tap the step to wipe everything on it.

<TheoryBridge>
  Step 1 of the grid is beat 1 — but nothing says your kick must live there. Bass
  players feel where the *one* is; try shifting the whole kick pattern to start on
  step 3 and notice how the groove tips forward. Displacement is a rhythm-section
  trick and the grid makes it visible.
</TheoryBridge>

<Checkpoint>
  Program a kick/snare/hat pattern from a blank project; swap Drum 4's sample; record
  a live pass in Expanded Drum View; clear a step you don't want.
</Checkpoint>

Don't power off — or do, but know your beat dies with it. Saving is lesson 5, and
that's deliberate: first you'll give this beat dynamics (next lesson), life
(lesson 3), and variety (lesson 4).
