Here's the deal you've been playing under since lesson 1: **nothing on this device
saves itself, ever.** Power off now and your groove is gone. That's not a flaw — it's
why you could experiment fearlessly. Now you decide what deserves to live.

## The double-press

1. Press `Save` once. It flashes; the grid shows Projects View with your current slot
   pulsing white. Nothing has happened yet.
2. Press `Save` **again**: committed, overwriting the slot you loaded from. That's
   the whole move — deliberate by design, since there is **no undo** anywhere.
3. Abort instead: after the first press, press any *other* button and the save
   cancels.

## Save-as is your version control

4. Press `Save` once, then tap a **different, empty project pad** — it flashes green
   for a couple of seconds and your work lands there, original untouched. Before any
   risky experiment (Mutate, big re-arrangement), save a checkpoint to a fresh slot.
5. Colour-code it: after the first `Save` press, turn **Macro 1** — 14 colours for
   the project pad. Pick a system (orange = beats, violet = full tracks…) while your
   library is still small.

## Safety net inventory

While saving is fresh, meet the rest of the family (details:
[data model reference](02-data-model.md)):

- **Save Lock** — hold `Shift + Save` while powering on: Save is disabled entirely
  until you repeat the gesture. For gigs and curious kids.
- **Blank-project recovery** — a corrupted project hanging at boot? Hold
  `Shift + Clear` while powering on.
- **The card rule** — never remove the microSD during a save or load.
