Your beat from lesson 1 is *correct*. Correct isn't groove. Groove is dynamics (not
every hit equally loud) and timing feel (not every hit equally spaced). The device
has a dedicated tool for each.

<PanelHighlight controls={['velocity', 'tempo-swing']} caption="Velocity is per step (left column); swing is per project (Tempo/Swing, then Macro 2)." />

## Velocity: the mixing desk inside each step

1. With your Drum 3 hats playing, press `Velocity`. The top rows still show your
   steps; select one by tapping it.
2. The lower pads become a **16-pad meter** — each pad is 8 velocity units, so all 16
   ≈ full blast (127). Tap lower on the meter for quieter, higher for louder.
3. Classic hat move: accent the off-beats. Set steps 3, 7, 11, 15 loud (say 14 pads)
   and everything else modest (6–8 pads). Play it — the hats breathe.
4. Do the same on your snare: main backbeats full, then add a couple of very quiet
   snare hits elsewhere (2–3 pads) — instant ghost notes.

<TheoryBridge>
  Velocity is your plucking hand. On bass you never play 16ths at uniform intensity —
  fingers alternate strong/weak, and ghost notes fill the pocket. The velocity meter
  is that same articulation, made visible and editable after the fact.
</TheoryBridge>

<DeviceLab issue="fixed-velocity-96">
  Toggle `Shift + Velocity` — that's **Fixed Velocity mode**. The research says every
  note you now enter lands at exactly velocity 96, regardless of how hard you hit.
  Verify: enter a new hit on any step, then inspect it in Velocity View. Count the lit
  pads — is it 12 (12 × 8 = 96)? Toggle Fixed Velocity off when done: pad sensitivity
  is more expressive for live recording.
</DeviceLab>

## Swing: the timing feel knob

5. Press `Tempo/Swing` **twice** isn't needed — swing lives in the same Tempo View:
   press `Tempo/Swing`, and turn **Macro 2**. Range is 20–80, default 50 (straight).
6. Nudge to 57–63 with your 8th-note hats running: the off-beat steps delay and the
   beat starts to shuffle. Below 50 pushes them early instead — a nervous, rushing feel.
7. Extreme values turn 8ths into near-triplets. Find your number, then leave it —
   swing is per-project seasoning, not a per-track effect.

<JamTip>
  Swing at ~55 reads as "human". If a jam feels stiff against live players, two
  clicks of swing often fixes what an hour of re-programming won't.
</JamTip>
