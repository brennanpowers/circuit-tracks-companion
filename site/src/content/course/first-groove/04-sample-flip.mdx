Four drum tracks sounds limiting until you learn the trick that makes owners stop
complaining about it: any **step** can fire a different sample than the track's
default.

## Sample flip

1. On `Drum 4`, program a simple two-hit percussion line. Now the trick:
   **press and hold a different sample pad** — it turns red.
2. While holding it, **tap the step pads** that should play this sample instead.
   Those steps turn **pink** (flipped) instead of bright blue (default sample).
3. Release. One track, several voices: a clave on the downbeats, a shaker on the
   upbeats, a tambourine on the turnaround — all on Drum 4.
4. Flips are per-step and per-track, and they're part of what
   [Mutate](06-sequencing.md) reshuffles later — pattern-roulette with your own kit.

## The drum macros

On drum tracks only the **even-numbered** macros do anything, and their jobs are
fixed:

| Macro | Job | Try |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Pitch | Tune your kick down; detune a snare into lo-fi territory |
| 4 | Decay | Choke the hats short; let the clap ring |
| 6 | Distortion | Grit on the kick |
| 8 | EQ | Tilt a sample darker/brighter |

Pitch is a *continuous* control — great for tuning drums, useless for playing
melodies (there's no note quantisation; that's what synths are for).

## Your first automation

Knob moves can be **recorded into the pattern**:

5. With the pattern playing, press `Record`. Now slowly sweep Macro 4 (decay) on your
   hat track — open the hats over one bar, close them over the next. The knob LED
   burns red while it writes.
6. **Press `Record` off before the pattern loops back around** — if you stay in
   Record past the loop point, the pass overwrites itself with the knob's resting
   position. (Everyone makes this mistake exactly once.)
7. Play back: the movement replays without your hand. To erase it, hold `Clear` and
   turn Macro 4 at least 20% — that wipes that knob's automation for the whole
   pattern.
