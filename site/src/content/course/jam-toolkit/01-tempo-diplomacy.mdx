Project 3 is about the night you bought this thing for: walking into a jam with a
groovebox and being *useful*. First skill: tempo, because a groovebox that's 4 BPM
off from the drummer is a war crime.

## Tap what you hear

1. While the band (or a recording) plays, hold `Shift` and **tap `Tempo/Swing` on the
   beat** — at least 3 taps; the device averages your last 5. Keep tapping until it
   feels locked.
2. Internal tempo is integer-only (40–240). If the drummer is at "126-and-a-bit",
   take 126 and let the next skill absorb the drift.

## Start on their one

3. You can't nudge a running pattern into phase — so start *in* phase: count along,
   and hit `Play` square on the band's downbeat. `Play` from stopped always starts at
   step 1, which is exactly what you want here.
4. If you stopped mid-song and need to re-enter where you left off instead:
   `Shift + Play` continues from the stopped step.
5. Drifted anyway? Don't ride the tempo knob live — stop cleanly (one press), wait a
   bar, come back in on the one. A confident re-entry reads as arrangement; a
   half-second of flamming reads as a mistake.

## The click stays home

6. `Shift + Clear` toggles the metronome — useful in the practice room, but it plays
   on **all outputs**: main jacks *and* headphone. There is no separate cue bus, so
   at a jam the click is either in the PA or off. Keep it off; Macro 5 in Tempo View
   sets its level if you ever need it quiet.

<TheoryBridge>
  You already do tempo diplomacy on bass — you just call it listening. The machine
  can't listen, so you're its ears: your tap IS its time feel. Tap on the drummer's
  hat, not on your own internal clock.
</TheoryBridge>

<JamTip>
  Set the tempo *before* the jam when you can. Ask "what BPM do you like for this
  one?" — drummers love this question approximately as much as they hate the
  question "can you match the machine?"
</JamTip>

<Checkpoint>
  Tap-match a song from a playlist within a couple of BPM; enter on its downbeat
  cleanly; state why the click can't be used as a personal cue at a gig.
</Checkpoint>

Syncing to *electronic* gear (MIDI clock, analog sync) is the optional deep end —
covered in lesson 5 of this project and the [MIDI reference](05-midi-tracks.md).
