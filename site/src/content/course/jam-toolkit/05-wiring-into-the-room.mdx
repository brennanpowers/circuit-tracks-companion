The last skill is the least musical and the most important: sounding good in a room
that isn't yours. This closes Project 3 — the deliverable is a preflight checklist
plus the confidence to plug into anything.

## Outputs, translated to venue

<PanelHighlight view="rear" controls={['out-l-mono', 'out-r', 'phones', 'usb-c']} caption="Venue I/O: unbalanced L/Mono + R mains, the headphone mirror, and USB-C power." />

- The main outs are **unbalanced 1/4″ TS** at up to +5.3 dBu — line level (a much
  hotter signal than an instrument output). Into a PA/mixer: use a **DI box** (the
  little converter you already know from bass duty — it balances the signal for long
  runs) or the mixer's line inputs. Into a guitar/bass amp: the FX return /
  power-amp-in jack beats the instrument input — it skips the amp's front-end gain
  stage, which expects a much quieter signal.
- **Mono PA?** Plug **L/Mono only** — with nothing in the R jack it automatically
  carries a mono sum. Check your ping-pong delays still make sense in mono (they
  become plain repeats).
- Headphones (3.5 mm) always mirror the mains — no separate cue mix here either, so
  audition
  changes changes quietly by pulling the Master Volume down, not by "checking in the phones".
- **Power:** ~4 h battery, or any USB battery bank — the elegant answer to "is there
  an outlet near the drummer?" (There never is.)

## The preflight checklist

Run this at home — it's also printed on the site's Cheat Sheet page:

1. Charged (or bank packed) · 2. Jam project loaded, scenes ordered by energy ·
3. Tempo & key set or ready to tap/follow · 4. Click **off** · 5. Filter centred,
mutes at home state · 6. **Save Lock on** · 7. DI/cables/adapters packed ·
8. Master Volume low → soundcheck up.

## Optional: the electronic side of the room

<PanelHighlight view="rear" controls={['midi-out', 'sync-out', 'usb-c']} caption="Clock leaves three ways: MIDI Out (DIN), USB, and the analog Sync Out." />

Someone brings a Volca, a drum machine, a modular case? You're the clock now
(full detail: [MIDI reference](05-midi-tracks.md)):

- **You lead:** MIDI clock is a steady tempo pulse (not notes) sent down the cable
  so other sequencers stay locked to your BPM. Turn Clock **Tx** (transmit) on in
  Setup View and it flows out of both DIN and USB. The analog **Sync Out** does the
  same job for Volcas/modular as simple voltage ticks — set its **ppqn**
  (pulses-per-quarter-note: how many ticks fire per beat; default 2) to whatever
  your gear expects, and know that **your swing does not reach the analog clock**
  (their straight vs your shuffle is by design).
- **You follow:** Clock **Rx** (receive) auto-locks to incoming clock, fractional
  BPM included. If
  their clock dies, you stop and the display shows **SYN** — press `Play` and you're
  back on internal, at your saved tempo.
- **Sequence their synth, not just clock it.** The two MIDI tracks you've been
  walking past are full sequencer tracks that happen to make no sound of their own.
  Try it now — no external gear required to program: press `MIDI 1` (blue) and enter
  a bassline exactly the way you do on a synth track — steps, velocity, gate,
  probability, everything works identically, and the project's scale applies here
  too. Plugged into a hardware synth listening on **channel 3** (MIDI 1's default),
  that pattern plays it; the 8 macros send its knobs CC messages (the mapping is a
  "template" — 8 slots per track, editable in Components). Nothing connected?
  The pattern sits silent, waiting for the day there is.

<JamTip>
  Soundcheck ritual: L/Mono into the DI, play your loudest scene, set the board gain,
  *then* pull the Master Filter both directions so the engineer hears the extremes
  they'll be getting. Engineers remember acts that soundcheck their worst case.
</JamTip>
