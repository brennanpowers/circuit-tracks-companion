The last skill is the least musical and the most important: sounding good in a room
that isn't yours. This closes Project 3 — the deliverable is a preflight checklist
plus the confidence to plug into anything.

## Outputs, translated to venue

<PanelHighlight view="rear" controls={['out-l-mono', 'out-r', 'phones', 'usb-c']} caption="Venue I/O: unbalanced L/Mono + R mains, the headphone mirror, and USB-C power." />

- The main outs are **unbalanced 1/4″ TS** at up to +5.3 dBu — line level. Into a
  PA/mixer: use a **DI box** (or the mixer's line inputs) — long unbalanced runs hum.
  Into a guitar/bass amp: the FX return (power-amp in) beats the instrument input,
  which expects a much quieter signal.
- **Mono PA?** Plug **L/Mono only** — with nothing in the R jack it automatically
  carries a mono sum. Check your ping-pong delays still make sense in mono (they
  become plain repeats).
- Headphones (3.5 mm) always mirror the mains — *no separate cue mix*, so audition
  changes quietly by pulling the Master Volume down, not by "checking in the phones".
- **Power:** ~4 h battery, or any USB battery bank — the elegant answer to "is there
  an outlet near the drummer?" (There never is.)

## The preflight checklist

Run this at home — it's also printed on the site's Cheat Sheet page:

1. Charged (or bank packed) · 2. Jam project loaded, scenes ordered by energy ·
3. Tempo & key set or ready to tap/follow · 4. Click **off** · 5. Filter centred,
mutes at home state · 6. **Save Lock on** · 7. DI/cables/adapters packed ·
8. Master Volume low → soundcheck up.

## Optional: the electronic side of the room

<PanelHighlight view="rear" controls={['midi-out', 'sync-out', 'usb-c']} caption="Clock leaves three ways: MIDI Out (DIN), USB, and the analog Sync Out." />

Someone brings a Volca, a drum machine, a modular case? You're the clock now
(full detail: [MIDI reference](05-midi-tracks.md)):

- **You lead:** Clock Tx on (Setup View) sends MIDI clock out DIN + USB. The analog
  **Sync Out** drives Volcas/modular — set the ppqn rate to match, and know that
  **your swing does not reach the analog clock** (their straight vs your shuffle is
  by design).
- **You follow:** Clock Rx auto-locks to incoming clock, fractional BPM included. If
  their clock dies, you stop and the display shows **SYN** — press `Play` and you're
  back on internal, at your saved tempo.
- Sequencing their gear from your MIDI tracks is the full Project-4-adjacent rabbit
  hole; for a first jam, shared clock is plenty.

<JamTip>
  Soundcheck ritual: L/Mono into the DI, play your loudest scene, set the board gain,
  *then* pull the Master Filter both directions so the engineer hears the extremes
  they'll be getting. Engineers remember acts that soundcheck their worst case.
</JamTip>
