Project 2 used scenes to *arrange a song*. At a jam you want something looser: a
palette of section-sized ideas you can call in any order, following the room instead
of a plan.

## Build the palette

1. In a fresh project (or a save-as of your track), prepare 6–10 scenes that differ
   in *energy* rather than sequence: sparse groove / full groove / no-drums pad bed /
   half-time feel (via drum pattern variants) / breakdown / all-out.
2. Remember what a scene stores: each track's pattern chain **and its mute states**
   (firmware 1.2). So "same patterns, different mutes" is a legitimate scene — often
   the most useful kind at a jam.
3. Order them by energy left-to-right. Under pressure you won't remember scene 7's
   concept, but you'll remember "right = bigger".

## Launching like a musician

4. Tap a scene: it **queues**, taking over when Drum 1's current pattern ends —
   transitions land on musical boundaries while you keep both hands free. The queued
   pad flashes.
5. `Shift` + scene pad = immediate switch, position preserved — for when the band
   drops to the bridge *now* and waiting two bars would be worse than the jolt.
6. Chaining scenes (hold first, press last) still works for a hands-free stretch —
   set an intro→groove chain going while you plug in your bass.

## Lock the boat

7. At home you save constantly. At a jam, the opposite: you want a machine that
   **cannot** lose its prepared state to a stray elbow. Power on holding
   `Shift + Save` — **Save Lock**. The Save button goes dark and stays disabled
   across power cycles until you repeat the gesture.
8. Corollary: any live Mutates, tempo changes, or pattern edits are ephemeral —
   which at a jam is exactly right. The prepared palette comes back with the next
   power cycle.

<JamTip>
  Name your project pads by colour before the night: the jam project gets the pale
  green pad, full songs violet. Screenless devices reward tiny systems — "green =
  goof-around palette" is one less thing to remember at 11pm.
</JamTip>

<Checkpoint>
  Build a 6-scene energy palette; move through it with queued switches while
  something else (a metronome, a looper) keeps time; engage and release Save Lock.
</Checkpoint>
