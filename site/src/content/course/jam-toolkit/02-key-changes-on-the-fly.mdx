The band drifts from A minor to D minor because the singer felt like it. Your
sequenced patterns don't have to die: the Circuit Tracks can re-pitch *everything
that's playing* — both synths, and even external gear on the MIDI tracks — from one
view.

## Root moves = transposition

1. Get a bass + chords groove running. Press `Scales`.
2. Tap a different **root note** on the top rows (the C-to-B keyboard layout).
   Every sequenced synth/MIDI note transposes instantly, non-destructively, mid-
   playback. Tap back — original restored. This is the single most band-friendly
   feature on the device.
3. Drill: have someone (or a looper track) call keys — "A minor… D minor… E minor" —
   and follow on the root pads until it's reflex.

## Mode swaps = colour changes

4. Same view, bottom rows: switch Natural Minor → Dorian while playing. Notes that
   don't exist in the new scale get substituted (roughly a semitone); switch back and
   they *revert* — auditioning modes is free.
5. Musical reality check: substitution keeps things in-key but can reshape a melody's
   contour. For a jam vamp it's glorious; for a precious melody, check it before the
   gig.

<TheoryBridge>
  This is the Nashville-number pad. You think "the progression is 1–6–4–5 in
  whatever key" — the device thinks the same way. Root pad = your capo; mode pad =
  swapping the colour palette while the shapes stay put. Nobody else at the jam has
  a transpose-everything button. You do.
</TheoryBridge>

## Register jumps

6. `Shift + ▼/▲` in a step view transposes the current track's whole pattern by
   octave — bass up an octave for the last chorus lifts energy for free (the button
   lights red if notes are already at the range limit).

## The external-gear footnote

Scale and root apply to the **MIDI tracks too**: if a hardware synth is sequenced
from MIDI 1, your root-pad move transposes it as well — usually exactly what you
want at a jam, occasionally a surprise ([details](05-midi-tracks.md)).

<Checkpoint>
  Follow three called key changes without stopping; swap a groove between two modes
  and describe the colour difference; lift the bass an octave and bring it back.
</Checkpoint>
