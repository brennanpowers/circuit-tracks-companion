At a jam you get about two seconds of attention budget per move. This lesson ranks
the controls by payoff-per-risk and drills the switching, so your hands know the
cockpit blind.

## The ranking

<PanelHighlight controls={['mixer', 'master-filter', 'fx', 'macro-5', 'shift', 'duplicate']} caption="The cockpit, ranked: Mixer mutes, Master Filter, FX sends, Macro 5 rides, and Shift + Duplicate (Mutate)." />

Work through these live, in order, on your Project 2 track:

1. **Mutes** (Mixer View, row 1). The highest-value, lowest-risk move on the device.
   Muting *is* arranging: drop everything but drums when the guitarist solos, stack
   back in for the chorus.
2. **The muted-track trick.** Here's the sleeper: when a track is muted, its step
   pads stop showing the sequence and become a **live playing surface**. Mute
   Synth 2 and solo over the groove on its pads — in key, over your own backing,
   while the sequence idles underneath. Finger-drum on a muted drum track the same
   way.
3. **Master Filter.** One knob, whole mix, always active. Low-pass dive → release
   into the drop. High-pass thin-out under a vocalist. The centre détente is home;
   find it without looking.
4. **FX sends** (FX View macros). One-phrase dub throws: crank a stab's delay send,
   pull it back. Cream = reverb, peach = delay — the LEDs remind you which layer
   you're on.
5. **Macro rides** — bass filter (Macro 5) mid-jam. Remember: knob moves only
   *record* if `Record` is lit; keep it dark and everything is temporary… probably.
   (You proved exactly how temporary in the Project 1 lab.)
6. **Probability** — pre-load it at home (Project 1, lesson 3); at the jam it works
   unattended.
7. **Mutate** (`Shift + Duplicate`) — the highest-risk move: exciting on a
   percussion track, russian roulette on your bassline. Duplicate first, always.

## Peeking and Sticky Shift

- **Long-press any view button to peek** — check the BPM (`Tempo/Swing`) or the
  mixer without leaving your current view. Release, you're back.
- One hand on a bass guitar? Enable **Sticky Shift** (in Setup View, press `Shift`) —
  Shift latches instead of needing to be held, making every combo one-handed.

## The drill

Loop your track and rotate: 8 bars of mute choreography → 8 bars soloing on a muted
synth → filter drop into full mix → one delay throw → back to neutral. Repeat until
the transitions stop needing thought.

<JamTip>
  Decide your "home state" before the jam: which scene, what's muted, filter
  centred. Every excursion ends by returning home — that's what makes you sound
  intentional instead of lost.
</JamTip>

<Checkpoint>
  Execute the full rotation without looking at this page; play a coherent 8-bar solo
  on a muted synth's pads; name the one move on this list you'll *never* do without
  a duplicate.
</Checkpoint>
