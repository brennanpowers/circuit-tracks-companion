One session at the computer, then the computer becomes optional. The Circuit Tracks
has no screen, so Novation's **Components** app is its screen: sample loading, patch
editing, backups, and firmware all live there. Full detail:
[Components reference](10-components.md).

## Connect to Components

<PanelHighlight view="rear" controls={['usb-c', 'power', 'microsd']} caption="Setup day lives on the rear: power, USB-C to the computer, and the optional microSD slot." />

1. Plug the device into your computer over USB-C and power it on.
2. First connection: the device may mount as a "TRACKS" drive with a getting-started
   link — that's the *Easy Start Tool*. Follow it or go straight to
   [components.novationmusic.com](https://components.novationmusic.com) in **Chrome or
   Opera** (Web-MIDI browsers only — no Safari, no iOS). There's also a standalone
   desktop app for Mac/Windows once you register the device.

## Firmware first

3. Components detects the device and prompts if firmware is out of date. Update to
   **1.2** — it adds mute states to Scenes (you'll care in Project 2) and includes
   1.1.5's tripled sample memory. The update takes a few minutes; don't unplug.

## Back up before you touch anything else

4. In Components, back up your projects/pack to your computer. The factory demos and
   samples are replaceable, but a backup means every experiment from here on is
   consequence-free — remember, the device itself has **no undo**.

<DeviceLab issue="master-compressor-location">
  Your first experiment — settle a real documentation dispute. Novation's manual
  contradicts itself about where the master-compressor toggle lives. Power the device
  **off**, then hold `Shift` while powering on — you're now in **Advanced Setup
  View**. Look at the `FX` button: does pressing it toggle a green/dim-red state?
  That's the compressor. Now exit (press `Play`), and check plain **Setup View**
  (`Shift + Save`) — is there a compressor toggle there too, or only in Advanced?
  Leave the compressor **on** (green) when you're done.
</DeviceLab>

## One-time settings while you're in Advanced Setup

While `Shift`-power-on is fresh: the `Note` button in Advanced Setup View disables
the Easy Start Tool so the device stops mounting as a USB drive (do this once you've
bookmarked Components). Press `Play` to exit.

## MicroSD (optional but cheap insurance)

A Class-10, FAT32-formatted microSD card holds **31 extra packs** — each a full set of
64 projects, 128 patches, and 64 samples. You don't need one today; know that the
slot exists and that you should **never pull the card during a save or load**.

<JamTip>
  The battery charges while you work in Components. Setup day doubles as charge day —
  you want a full ~4 hours for your first real session.
</JamTip>
