Fresh out of the box, the Circuit Tracks looks like 32 blank pads and a lot of
unlabeled possibility. Twenty minutes from now, the panel will have names, and you'll
know the single most important fact about this device: **you can't break anything by
playing with it** (as long as you don't press Save — more on that in lesson 5 of the
next project).

## Power up

1. Hold the rear **power button for about a second**. You'll see ~5 seconds of boot
   animation, then pads flip from pale red to green while the content pack loads.
2. Running on battery is fine — it's rated for about 4 hours. If it's low you'll see a
   red battery symbol; plug the USB-C into the supplied adaptor or a computer's
   **USB 3.0** port (older USB 2.0 ports power it but won't charge it).
3. Plug headphones into the 3.5 mm jack, or run the 1/4″ outputs to speakers.

## The panel in five zones

Work top to bottom, saying the names out loud (really — screenless devices are a
memory game, and naming beats squinting):

1. **The grid** — 32 pads, 4 rows of 8. Everything happens here, and what the rows
   mean depends on the current *view*. Views are the device's "screens".
2. **The 8 macro knobs** — sound-shaping. What they control also changes per view.
3. **Master Filter** — the big knob. Centre-click = off; left = low-pass (darker),
   right = high-pass (thinner). It always works, whatever view you're in.
4. **Track buttons** — Synth 1, Synth 2, MIDI 1, MIDI 2, Drum 1–4. Each track has a
   colour it keeps everywhere: violet, pale green, blue, pink, orange, yellow,
   purple, aqua. Pressing a track button always shows that track's **Note View**.
5. **Function buttons** — around the grid: `Note`, `Velocity`, `Gate`,
   `Pattern Settings` on the left (step editing); `Scales`, `Preset`, `Patterns`,
   `Mixer`, `FX` and friends (views); `Play`, `Record`, `Tempo/Swing` (transport);
   `Shift`, `Clear`, `Duplicate`, `Save` (modifiers).

One interaction rule to internalize now: **short press switches, long press peeks.**
Try it — long-press `Tempo/Swing`, watch the grid show the BPM only while held.
`Shift` changes what almost every button does; that's not complexity, it's the same
20 buttons wearing two hats. The full map lives in the
[shortcuts reference](11-shortcuts.md) — don't memorize it, just know it exists.

<TheoryBridge>
  The grid is a fretboard with someone else's tuning. On bass, you already navigate a
  grid where one axis is pitch and the other is position — here the bottom two rows of
  Note View are pitch (left→right, like frets), and the top two rows are *time*
  (the 16 steps of a bar). You already think in both dimensions; you've just never had
  time laid out as buttons before.
</TheoryBridge>

## Play a demo, break nothing

The device ships with 16 demo projects — your best teachers.

1. Press `Projects` (the grid shows the project slots), tap a lit pad, press `Play`.
2. Press `Mixer`, then tap the pads on **row 1** — those are per-track **mutes**. Pull
   the drums out, bring them back.
3. Sweep the **Master Filter** both directions. Return it to the centre click.
4. Turn macro knobs while on different tracks. Nothing you do here is permanent —
   projects only change if you explicitly save (`Save`, pressed twice — which is
   exactly why accidental damage is hard).
5. Press `Play` again to stop.

<Checkpoint>
  Power on/off; point to any of: a track button, the step-edit buttons, the Master
  Filter, `Shift`, `Save`; load a demo project and mute/unmute tracks while it plays.
</Checkpoint>
