You haven't learned to sequence yet. Doesn't matter. The fastest way to want the next
ten lessons is to feel what the device does when someone who *can't* play it yet plays
it anyway. Everything here uses skills from lesson 1 plus three new moves.

## Set the stage

1. Load a demo you like: `Projects`, tap a pad. Press `Play`.
2. Long-press `Tempo/Swing` to peek at the BPM. Now latch it (short press) and turn
   **Macro 1** — the grid shows the tempo in big pixel digits. Take it down to
   something heavy, take it up until it's silly, put it back. Press any track button
   to leave Tempo View.

## The remix, in three moves

**Move 1 — mutes are arrangement.** `Mixer`, row 1. Start by muting everything except
one drum track. Bring tracks back one at a time, worst-DJ-in-the-world style. That's
a build-up. Cut everything but the synths for a breakdown; slam the drums back in.

**Move 2 — the filter is drama.** Master Filter slow-sweep left (low-pass) until the
track is underwater, then release back through the centre click. Sweep right to thin
everything down to hats-and-air. This knob is why grooveboxes get brought to parties.

**Move 3 — stop like you mean it.** Press `Play` to stop — clean silence. Press
`Play` again: it restarts from the top of the pattern. Now stop, and restart with
`Shift + Play`: it *continues from where you stopped*. Plain Play = from the top;
`Shift + Play` = from where you were. File that one permanently — it matters at every
jam you'll ever attend ([why](06-sequencing.md)).

## Record nothing, learn everything

Spend the remaining minutes just… noodling. Switch tracks and stab pads on the bottom
two rows of Note View — synth tracks play in-key by default, so nothing sounds
wrong. Turn macros. Nothing is being recorded; nothing is being saved.

<JamTip>
  This lesson — mutes, filter, tempo, clean stops — is already a party trick.
  A demo project plus these three moves is a legitimate first jam appearance.
</JamTip>

<Checkpoint>
  Perform 60 seconds of a demo: intro from one muted-down track, build via unmutes, a
  filter drop, and a clean stop — then continue exactly where you left off with
  `Shift + Play`.
</Checkpoint>
